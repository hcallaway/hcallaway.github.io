Why I am a great Product Manager

I am an eternal empath. I think of things from other's perspectives, and that gives me the unique capability to tune a conversation appropriately for the audience, regardless of how technical it may be. I am also very big-pictured focused, while remaining a realist. The ultiamte project manager, I think of big dreams for each software, but have the experience and knowledge to know that world domination, much like Rome, can't be built in a day. One of my best PM qualities is thinking adjacent to the problem (and/or solution). This gives me the ability to see downstream effects that may benefit, or impede, a product or project, and plan accordingly. It also makes me a great "we should include Houston in this meeting" value, just because I tend to think outside the box as a default. Not to mention all the opportunities it gives the other meeting attendees to say "Houston we have a problem."
